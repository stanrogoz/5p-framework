Push

Challenge the assumptions.

Now apply pressure. Interrogate the reasoning, surface hidden assumptions, and demand evidence so weak ideas break here — not in front of stakeholders.

  • Ask “what would have to be true?”
  • Request counter-arguments and failure modes.
  • Check claims, sources, and edge cases.

Worked example — Defending a work item your manager critiqued

Evaluate options: Full defence assumes the critique is about the design — it's often about a hidden constraint (budget, deadline). Instant concession signals no conviction, usually they want judgment; folding in one message tells them either the original design had no reasoning behind it, or you'll abandon good reasoning under light pressure.

The surviving move: Combines options 3 and 4, first a diagnostic question, then concede the constraint got buried in the doc, redraft, highlight the constraint, show conviction, not stubbornness.
