Open up the space.

Go wide before you go deep. Ask for several angles, options, and analogies so you explore real alternatives instead of latching onto the first plausible answer.

  • Ask for several distinct options or framings.
  • Invite unconventional and contrarian takes.
  • Stay divergent — judge later, not now.

Worked example — Defending a work item your manager critiqued

Full defence: walk through the rationale point by point, explain why you designed it this way.

Immediate concession: "fair, I'll simplify it right away."

Diagnose first: ask one question to find what's actually driving the critique (complexity? timeline? cost? a stakeholder in their ear?).

Blame the packaging: the doc failed to show the requirement that forces the complexity; the miss was communication, not engineering.
