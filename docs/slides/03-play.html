Open up the space.

Go wide before you go deep. Ask for several angles, options, and analogies so you explore real alternatives instead of latching onto the first plausible answer.

  • Ask for several distinct options or framings.
  • Invite unconventional and contrarian takes.
  • Stay divergent — judge later, not now.

Worked example — Picking a topic for a 15-minute meetup talk

The war story: "the outage that took us down for 6 hours and what we changed."

The how-to: a practical walkthrough of a tool or pattern you use daily.

The opinion piece: "why we stopped doing X that everyone says you should do."

The failure talk: a project you shut-down, the hard decision that saved a quarter.
