Prepare for scrutiny.

Get ready to defend it. Anticipate the hard questions, name the risks and trade-offs, and have your backup reasoning ready for the room.

  • List the likely objections and answers.
  • Be explicit about risks and trade-offs.
  • Critique the work — show how you got here.

Worked example — Defending a work item your manager critiqued

"Is the constraint even valid?" Your reply never takes a position. Your manager leaves the exchange not knowing what you actually think.

"Right away"This commits your time before the requirement is even confirmed valid.

"What else did you forget?" Forgetting a design-driving constraint invites doubt about the rest of the doc.
