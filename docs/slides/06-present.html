Prepare for scrutiny.

Get ready to defend it. Anticipate the hard questions, name the risks and trade-offs, and have your backup reasoning ready for the room.

  • List the likely objections and answers.
  • Be explicit about risks and trade-offs.
  • Critique the work — show how you got here.

Worked example — Picking a topic for a 15-minute meetup talk/p>

Objection 1: "Couldn't you have known earlier?" Answer: yes — that's criterion #2, and here's the date we should have caught it.

Objection 2: "Does this make you look flaky?"Answer: shutting down bad projects fast is the brand, own your failures.

Objection 3: "Isn't a how-to talk less risky?" Answer: the how-to is solid but forgettable, and forgettable fails the "three people come talk to you" test.
