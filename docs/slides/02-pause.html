Pause

Plan the objective, add context.

Before you prompt, get clear on what a good answer even looks like. Define the real decision, the audience, and the constraints — then load the AI with the context it needs.

  • State the decision and what “done” looks like.
  • Add the constraints, data, and audience.
  • Choose the format you actually need back.

Worked example — Defending a work item your manager critiqued

The request: You shipped a piece of work — say, a design doc for a new internal service. Your manager comments in the review: "This feels over-engineered. I'm not sure we should build this.”

The frame: The outcome is not "win the argument." It's: defend, adapt, or concede — and respond in a way that raises your manager's confidence in your judgment either way.
